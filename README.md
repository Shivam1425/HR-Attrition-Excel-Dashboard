# HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard

An interactive Excel dashboard project focused on analyzing employee attrition patterns and identifying the business factors most strongly associated with workforce exits.

This project was built to help answer practical HR questions such as:

- Which employee groups contribute most to attrition?
- Is attrition concentrated in certain departments or job roles?
- How are salary hikes, job satisfaction, and tenure linked to employee exits?
- Which employee segments should HR prioritize for retention efforts?

## Project Objective

The goal of this project is to convert HR employee data into an interactive Excel dashboard that supports retention-focused decision-making.

The dashboard is designed to help stakeholders:

- monitor overall attrition levels
- identify high-risk employee segments
- understand role, tenure, and salary-related attrition patterns
- support targeted HR interventions and workforce planning

---

## Dataset Overview

- Dataset size: about `1,470` employees
- Domain: HR / workforce analytics
- Analysis focus:
  - employee attrition
  - department contribution

---

## Key Business Insights

1. Employees with `0-2 years` of tenure contribute the largest share of attrition, suggesting early-stage retention issues.
2. The `25-34` age group shows the highest attrition concentration.
3. `Sales Executives` and `Laboratory Technicians` are among the most attrition-prone job roles.
4. Employees receiving lower salary hikes show higher attrition levels, indicating possible compensation dissatisfaction.
5. Lower average job satisfaction aligns with higher attrition risk.
6. Department-level patterns show attrition is concentrated more heavily in specific business areas.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Strengthen onboarding and first-year engagement programs.
- Create targeted retention plans for high-risk roles and departments.
- Review compensation progression for employees receiving lower salary hikes.
- Improve employee feedback systems and management practices to address satisfaction gaps.
- Align career growth planning with role expectations and employee development paths.
